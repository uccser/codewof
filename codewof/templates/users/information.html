CodeWOF - Information Sheet for participants

Kia ora {{ user.first_name }}!

You are invited to participate in a research study on user engagement in CodeWOF. This study is being conducted by Kush Desai from the University of Canterbury – Te Whare Wānanga o Waitaha (UC). The research team also includes Tim Bell. The study is being carried out as a requirement for SENG402.

What is the purpose of this research?

This research aims to investigate how to provide sustainable, up-to-date web-based Python programming resources for teachers and students, and how gamification can be used to increase engagement with the CodeWOF platform. The information from this study will help inform improvements to CodeWOF, contributing to better programming education and supporting both teachers’ and students’ learning needs.

Why have you received this invitation?

You are invited to participate in this research because you are either a high school teacher or a student enrolled in COSC121 at the University of Canterbury. The study is being advertised to these groups to help evaluate and improve CodeWOF.

Your participation is voluntary

Your participation in this research is entirely your choice. If you decide not to participate, there are no consequences. Your decision will not affect your relationship with the University of Canterbury, the research team, or your course in any way.

What is involved in participating?

If you choose to take part in this research, you will use the CodeWOF website as you normally would to practice programming skills. Your anonymised usage data (such as time on site, questions completed, and types of questions attempted) will be collected while you use the website. Participation does not require any additional time beyond your normal use of the website, and you may spend as much or as little time as you wish.

Are there any potential benefits from taking part in this research?

A potential benefit is that you may improve your programming skills by using CodeWOF. The research will also contribute to enhancing programming education resources for the community.

Are there any potential risks involved in this research?

We do not anticipate any risks to participants from taking part in this research. All data collected will be anonymised, and only aggregated, anonymised results will be reported. No identifiable information will be published or shared with third parties. You do not need to be concerned about your results, as no-one is evaluating your particular work in CodeWOF.

What if you change your mind during or after the study?

You are free to withdraw from the study at any time. You can do this either by not using the CodeWOF website or by selecting an opt-out option here. Any data collected up to the point of withdrawal will be removed if still identifiable and possible. Once data analysis has started and individual data cannot be separated, removal will not be possible, but you always retain the right to ask questions or raise concerns about your data.

What will happen to the information you provide?

All data collected will be anonymised and stored securely on University of Canterbury servers, in password-protected files accessible only to the research team. No identifying information will be linked to your data, and individual participants will not be identifiable in any publication or report. Data will be destroyed after completion of the project.

Will the results of the study be published?

The results of this research will not be made public, they will only be used as part of my SENG402 research report.

Who can you contact if you have any questions or concerns?

If you have any questions about the research, please contact:

  • Kush Desai (Principal Researcher): kde54@uclive.ac.nz
  • Supervising staff member: Tim Bell, tim.bell@canterbury.ac.nz

If you have concerns or complaints about this research, please contact the Chair of the HREC at human-ethics@canterbury.ac.nz.

What happens next?

Please read this information and consider whether you wish to participate. By continuing to use the CodeWOF website and not selecting the opt-out option, you consent to take part in the anonymised data collection for this study.

If you would like a summary of the results or have further questions, please feel free to contact the research team.
